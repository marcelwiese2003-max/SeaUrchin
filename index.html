Why SeaUrchin

Mauritius's most
certified dive school

Under German management since 2006, SeaUrchin is the only SSI Diamond Instructor Trainer Center in Mauritius — and the island's sole female-owned diving school. We hold SSI, PADI, and CMAS certifications simultaneously.

Groups are capped at six divers maximum so every guest receives genuine personal attention. Our philosophy: individual, highly professional, and with total commitment to the environment.

Instruction in English, German, French, and Danish. Post-dive, you'll find hot beverages, showers, and good conversation waiting.

A Day at SeaUrchin

What a dive
day looks like

We operate Monday to Saturday, all year round. Two-tank dives run in the early morning so you have the afternoon for the island. Here's what to expect from the moment you arrive to the stories you'll tell that evening.

Standard divers: Meet at 8:00 am, depart 8:15 am

Beginners & refresher: Meet at 7:15 am

Arrive 30 minutes early to check in & collect gear

7:15

Arrival & Check-In

Present your certification card and logbook. Complete paperwork and collect your equipment from the center.

8:00

Morning Briefing

Your guide runs through the dive plan, conditions, safety procedures, and what marine life to watch for. Tanks load onto the pickup and head to Flic en Flac port.

8:15

On the Water

Assemble your gear on board. The boat carries 10, 12, or 15L steel cylinders and 12L aluminium tanks. Entry via roll-back — the classic way.

~Dive

The Dive

All dives follow Mauritian regulations with a certified guide. A mandatory 3-minute safety stop before surfacing. Boat and center carry oxygen, water, and First Aid kits.

~Return

Post-Dive

Disassemble rental equipment, rinse all gear in provided basins. Hot beverages and logbook time back at the center. The dive never really ends here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you
need to know

What should I bring?
A swimsuit and towel are compulsory. You're welcome to bring your own diving or snorkelling gear, but all necessary equipment is provided at the center.
When do you operate?
We dive Monday to Saturday, all year round. The center is open 8:00 – 16:00. We close on Sundays and January 1st only.
Do I need a medical certificate?
A medical certificate is not legally required in Mauritius. However, if you have any health concerns, we recommend consulting your doctor before travelling.
At what age can children dive?
Children can join playful water exercises from age 8. From age 10, they can work toward the Junior Open Water Diver certification. Rescue Diver training starts at 16 and DivePro training at 18.
How long does an Open Water certification take?
A minimum of four days is required for the full Open Water Diver certification — covering theory, confined water exercises, and four open water dives plus a written exam.
What tanks and valves do you use?
We accommodate both DIN and INT/Yoke valve fittings. We dive with 10, 12, and 15-litre steel cylinders, as well as 12-litre aluminium tanks.
What's the water temperature?
In summer (November – April), the water reaches up to 30 °C. In winter (May – October), it drops to around 24 °C. A 3mm wetsuit is comfortable year-round; a 5mm is available for cooler months.
Are there sharks?
Sharks are present in Mauritian waters — encounters are rare but possible. In winter, whales pass through while some shark species retreat deeper. All dives are fully guided for your safety.
How long before flying after a dive?
We recommend a surface interval of 18 – 24 hours before boarding a flight. Please plan your last diving day accordingly.
Can I dive to other coasts?
Yes — dive safaris to southern Mauritius are available for groups of minimum four people with advance reservation. Ask us to organise your island-wide adventure.
I'm pregnant — can I dive?
Diving during pregnancy is not permitted. Diving during breastfeeding, menstruation, or with silicone implants presents no issue whatsoever.
Can I switch between PADI and SSI?
Absolutely. Both SSI and PADI are ISO-certified standards and crossover between them is straightforward. We can assist with the process regardless of where you trained originally.
Are there night dives?
Yes, night dives are available subject to demand, weather conditions, and tides. Priced at €80 per person. Ask when you book.
What facilities are available?
We provide hot showers, equipment storage, logbook tables, hot beverages after dives, and a friendly, relaxed atmosphere to debrief the day's underwater highlights.
